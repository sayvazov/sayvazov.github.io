    Transportation

    This comes as no surprise, but we strongly recommend you use public transit to get around NYC. The Greenpoint Avenue stop of the G train is about a 5 - 10 minute walk away. This will take you most of downtown Brooklyn, and connects to approximately every other subway line in the city. Fares on all NYC trains and busses (but not the airport AirTrain, grrr) can be accessed by contact-less credit cards, Apple Pay, Google Pay, and similar.

    If you have never used the NYC subway, and are nervous about doing so, please reach out to us! We'd love to help you plan your trip - provided you do so before that weekend. For the most part, Google Maps is stellar about NYC transit, including live arrival times for trains and busses, up to the minute service changes, and every time Sasha thinks he can outsmart it, he finds himself incorrect. For those who may be concerned: crime on city transit, especially in central areas like Greenpoint, is incredibly rare. You are very likely in more danger driving to the airport in your hometown than you are sitting on the subway. If you are lost and confused and stuck somewhere underground - ask a stranger for help. New Yorkers are, as stereotypes suggest, more brusque and to the point than most Americans, but cannot resist showing off how much they know about getting around the city. And if they tell you wrong, someone (or 5 someones) will jump in to correct them.

    If you prefer, the NYC ferry runs every 50 minutes on weekends from lower Manhattan and Midtown. At the moment, the Greenpoint stop of the ferry is closed for repairs, but they are promising it will open by May. Realistically, though, the North Williamsburg stop is a 15-20 minute walk along the water from Greenpoint Loft.

    A Note for Drivers: The yearly 5 Boro Bike Tour is happeneing the same day as the wedding.
